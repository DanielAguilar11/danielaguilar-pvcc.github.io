    Introduction

    Article VIII of the Constitution of the Commonwealth of Virginia pertains to education. This article creates the framework for the state's educational system. It provides the rules and regulations governing the educational system of the state. With a focus on accessibility, local sovereignty, and the allocation of resources to guarantee excellent education for all people. This article is divided into 11 sections, which will be presented using the information that the Virginia Law page provides us.

  • Virginia Law

    • Section 1. Public schools of high quality to be maintained.

    In order to ensure that all children in the Commonwealth who are of school age have access to a free public elementary and secondary education, the General Assembly must design and sustain a high-quality educational program.

    Section 2. Standards of quality; State and local support of public schools.

    The Board of Education shall periodically set and establish quality standards for the various school divisions, and only the General Assembly may revise these standards. The General Assembly must decide how to allocate funds for maintaining educational programs that meet the required standards of quality and how to split the cost of such programs between the Commonwealth and the local units of government that make up such school divisions. Each local government entity is responsible for covering its own share of the expense through local taxes or other available resources.

    Section 3. Compulsory education; free textbooks.

    Every kid who is eligible and of legal age must get an obligatory elementary and secondary education, according to a provision made by the General Assembly. It will make sure that every child enrolled in a public school whose parent or guardian cannot afford to buy them receives free textbooks.

    Section 4. Board of Education.

    A nine-member Board of Education, to be chosen by the Governor and confirmed by the General Assembly, shall have overall control over the public education system. Except for those made to replace vacancies, all appointments must be for a period of four years. No more than three regular appointments may be booked in a single year due to the staggered terms.

    Section 5. Powers and duties of the Board of Education.

    This is what the Virginia Law page states: "The powers and duties of the Board of Education shall be as follows:"

      (a) Subject to such criteria and conditions as the General Assembly may prescribe, the Board shall divide the Commonwealth into school divisions of such geographical area and school-age population as will promote the realization of the prescribed standards of quality, and shall periodically review the adequacy of existing school divisions for this purpose.

      (b) It shall make annual reports to the Governor and the General Assembly concerning the condition and needs of public education in the Commonwealth, and shall in such report identify any school divisions which have failed to establish and maintain schools meeting the prescribed standards of quality.

      (c) It shall certify to the school board of each division a list of qualified persons for the office of division superintendent of schools, one of whom shall be selected to fill the post by the division school board. In the event a division school board fails to select a division superintendent within the time prescribed by law, the Board of Education shall appoint him.

      (d) It shall have authority to approve textbooks and instructional aids and materials for use in courses in the public schools of the Commonwealth.

      (e) Subject to the ultimate authority of the General Assembly, the Board shall have primary responsibility and authority for effectuating the educational policy set forth in this article, and it shall have such other powers and duties as may be prescribed by law.

    Section 6. Superintendent of Public Instruction.

    An experienced educator must serve as the Superintendent of Public Instruction, who will be appointed by the Governor and confirmed by the General Assembly for a term that coincides with that of the Governor making the appointment. However, the General Assembly may change the selection process and term of office by statute. Law must specify the Superintendent's responsibilities and authority.

    Section 7. School boards.

    A school board, to be made up of members chosen in the way, for the period, with the qualifications, and to the number prescribed by law, shall be responsible for overseeing the schools in each school division.

    Section 8. The Literary Fund.

    The proceeds from all public lands donated by Congress for free public school purposes, all escheated property, all waste and unappropriated lands, all property accruing to the Commonwealth by forfeiture, except as hereinafter provided, all fines collected for offenses committed against the Commonwealth, and the annual interest on the Literary Fund shall be set aside by the General Assembly as a permanent and perpetual school fund. However, the General Assembly may reserve all or any portion of extra funds received into the Fund's principal for public education purposes, including the teachers retirement fund, as long as the Fund's principal does not exceed $80,000,000.

    The proceeds from the sale of all property seized and forfeited to the Commonwealth due to a violation of the criminal laws of this Commonwealth prohibiting the manufacture, sale, or distribution of marijuana or controlled substances may be exempt from this section by general law provided by the General Assembly. Such revenues must be transferred to the state treasury and disbursed in accordance with the law in order to support law enforcement.

    The Board of Education will hold and manage the Literary Fund in accordance with any legal requirements. The Literary Fund's assets may be used as collateral for loans approved by the General Assembly, which would not entail the Commonwealth's full faith and credit.Assets held by the Fund in other funds or agencies that are repayable to the Fund are included in the Fund's principal.

    The amendment ratified November 6, 1990 and effective January 1, 1991—In paragraph one, after "forfeiture", added "except as hereinafter provided". Added a new paragraph after paragraph one. (Virginia Law).

    Section 9. Other educational institutions.

    Any educational institutions that are desired for the intellectual, cultural, and professional advancement of the people of this Commonwealth may be established, maintained, and operated by the General Assembly. The status and authority of such institutions' boards of visitors or other governing bodies should be as specified by law.

    Section 10. State appropriations prohibited to schools or institutions of learning not owned or exclusively controlled by the State or some subdivision thereof; exceptions to rule.

    In accordance with Virginia Law page, "No appropriation of public funds shall be made to any school or institution of learning not owned or exclusively controlled by the State or some political subdivision thereof; provided, first, that the General Assembly may, and the governing bodies of the several counties, cities and towns may, subject to such limitations as may be imposed by the General Assembly, appropriate funds for educational purposes which may be expended in furtherance of elementary, secondary, collegiate or graduate education of Virginia students in public and nonsectarian private schools and institutions of learning, in addition to those owned or exclusively controlled by the State or any such county, city or town; second, that the General Assembly may appropriate funds to an agency, or to a school or institution of learning owned or controlled by an agency, created and established by two or more States under a joint agreement to which this State is a party for the purpose of providing educational facilities for the citizens of the several States joining in such agreement; third, that counties, cities, towns, and districts may make appropriations to nonsectarian schools of manual, industrial, or technical training, and also to any school or institution of learning owned or exclusively controlled by such county, city, town, or school district."

    Section 11. Aid to nonpublic higher education.

    The General Assembly may provide for loans to, and grants to or on behalf of, students attending nonprofit institutions of higher education in the Commonwealth whose primary purpose is to provide collegiate or graduate education and not to provide religious training or theological education. The General Assembly may also provide for a State agency or authority to assist in borrowing money for construction of educational facilities at such institutions, provided that the Commonwealth shall not be liable for any debt created by such borrowing. The General Assembly may also provide for the Commonwealth or any political subdivision thereof to contract with such institutions for the provision of educational or other related services.

    The amendment ratified November 5, 1974 and effective January 1, 1975—Provided for "grants to or on behalf of" in addition to loans to students, in the first sentence. Added the last sentence to permit "the Commonwealth or any political subdivision thereof to contract with" nonprofit institutions of higher education. (Virginia Law).
